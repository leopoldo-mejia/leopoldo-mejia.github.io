Research Directions

Methods Development

We develop methods to investigate the electronic structure and dynamics of molecules, nanostructures, and materials. Using the many-body Green's function formalism, we study ground and excited states, quasiparticle dynamics, and out-of-equilibrium phenomena from first principles. For large systems, such as extensive nanostructures or materials with large unit cells, we incorporate stochastic methods to reduce computational scaling with system size, enabling calculations on systems with hundreds to thousands of electrons.

Nano-Electronics

We investigate charge and spin transport in molecules and nanostructures connected to macroscopic electrodes under external stimuli, such as bias voltages, electromagnetic fields, and mechanical forces. Our research seeks to uncover the fundamental quantum-mechanical effects driving these phenomena and to propose applications in nanoelectronics and spectroscopy. By combining in-house software with open-source tools, we approach these challenges from a multidisciplinary perspective.

Nanostructures & Materials

We study the fundamental electronic properties of materials and nanostructures and their potential applications. This includes the investigation of their electronic structure, quasiparticle dynamics, and interactions with electromagnetic fields. We focus on developing strategies to control these properties and contrast our computations with experimental observations.
